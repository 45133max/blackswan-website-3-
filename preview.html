THE PROBLEM

Workplace safety has become complicated — and the training doesn't stick.

Decision makers carry more duties every year, while employees forget most of what they're taught. Three problems, one root cause.

01

The rules won't sit still.

EU, national and company-level duties change constantly — NIS2, AI Act, DORA. Every update lands on your desk, whether you saw it coming or not.

€35M / 7%

maximum fines under the new EU regime landing 2025–26.

  • 8+ regulatory lines apply to a single mid-size site at once.
  • Since 12/2025, training duties are personal and non-delegable for management.
02

Training is forgotten within days.

Slide-based sessions document attendance, not competence. Knowledge fades long before the moment it's actually needed on the floor.

~90%

of mandatory training is forgotten within 24 hours (Ebbinghaus forgetting curve).

  • 730,000+ reportable workplace accidents in Germany in 2025 — about 2,000 a day.
  • In an emergency, only trained reaction chains perform — not memories of slides.
03

And you must prove all of it.

Courses are rebuilt by hand for every role and every rule change — with attendance lists as the only evidence when auditors ask.

73%

of analysed fine cases trace back to missing training or supervision (106 of 145).

  • Safety & HR teams lose ~120 hours a year tracking obligations by hand.
  • "Who knew what, when?" — attendance sheets can't answer it.
